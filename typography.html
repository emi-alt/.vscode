Tissues Plant and Animal tissues

A group of cells that are similar in structure and/or work together to achieve a particular fuunction forma a tissue

It is of two types - Plant tissue and Animal tissue


1. Plant tissues


Types of plant tissues

  1. 1.a Meristematic tissue
  2. 1.b Permanent tissue

Types of Meristematic tissues

  • 1.a.1 Apical
  • 1.a.2 Intercalary
  • 1.a.3 Lateral

1.a.1 Apical meristem

Apical meristem is present at the growing roots and increases the length of the root.

1.a.2 Intercalary meristem

Intercalary meristem seen in some plants is located near the node

(from where the flowers and leaves grow)

1.a.3 Lateral

The girth of the stem or root increases due to lateral meristem.

Types of Permanent tissues

  • 1.b.1 Simple permanent tissue
  • 1.b.2 Complex permanent tissue

1.b.1 Simple permanent tissue

Simple permanent tissues are characterized by having cells that are structurally and functionally similar, meaning they are made up of only one type of cell.

They are also known as homogeneous tissues.

1.b.2 Complex permanent tissue

Complex permanent tissue is a group of cells that perform a common function and are made up of different types of cells. These tissues are found in plants and are also known as vascular tissues.
